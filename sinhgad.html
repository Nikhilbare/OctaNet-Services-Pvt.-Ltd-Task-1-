Information

Sinhagad Fort, also known as Sinhagarh Fort, is a historical fortification located near Pune, Maharashtra, India. It is situated atop a hill called Sinhagad, which is part of the Bhuleshwar range of the Sahyadri Mountains. The fortification has a long and storied history, and it has been the site of several important battles over the years. It was originally built in the 17th century by the Maratha warrior king, Shivaji, as part of his campaign to establish a Maratha empire in India. Sinhagad Fort is known for its impressive architecture, which is a blend of Maratha and Mughal styles. The fortification features several impressive features, including a massive entrance gate, bastions, and a beautiful temple dedicated to the goddess Kali. Today, Sinhagad Fort is a popular tourist attraction in Pune, and it is known for its beautiful views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill to explore the fortification and enjoy the stunning scenery. There are also several trekking trails that lead to the fort, making it a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts. Sinhagad Fort is also known for its cultural significance, as it played an important role in the history of the Maratha Empire. The fortification has been the site of several important battles, including the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, which saw the Maratha forces defeat the Mughal army.
